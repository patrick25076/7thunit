Featured roles

We're hiring candidates who are hungry to make their impact across AI, engineering, and operations.

Hardware

Mechanical Design Engineer

You design and iterate on the physical structure of the robot - chassis, joints, mounting systems, and mechanical assemblies. You own CAD, tolerance analysis, and component selection from early concept through prototype. You work closely with the power electronics and manufacturing engineers to ensure designs can be built with real components under real constraints. SolidWorks or Fusion 360 experience required. To apply, use the form below.

Power Electronics Engineer

You design and own the electrical architecture of the robot - power distribution, motor drivers, battery management, PCB design, and wiring. Without this role nothing runs. You work closely with the mechanical engineer on physical layout and with the embedded engineer on control interfaces. Experience with DC motor systems, battery architectures, and PCB design in hardware-constrained environments required. To apply, use the form below.

Manufacturing & Prototyping Engineer

You turn designs into physical reality. You source components, manage supplier relationships, oversee prototype assembly, and iterate fast when parts don't fit or don't work. You know the difference between what looks good in CAD and what can actually be built, and you say so early. Experience with rapid prototyping, off-the-shelf component sourcing, and hands-on assembly of electromechanical systems required. To apply, use the form below.

Systems & Integration

Embedded Systems Engineer

You own the low-level software that makes the robot operate in the real world. You write firmware for microcontrollers, design real-time control loops, handle motor drivers and sensor interfaces, and ensure reliable communication between hardware and software. You are the bridge between the physical robot and everything running above it. We work with ARM-based microcontrollers, ROS 2, and custom motor control architectures. Prior experience with embedded C/C++, RTOS, and hardware-software integration required. To apply, use the form below.

Systems Integration Engineer

You make sure all subsystems - mechanical, electrical, firmware, and software - work together as a single coherent robot. You define interfaces between teams, design and run integration tests, and debug the hardest cross-system problems that no one else sees because they only look at their own domain. Prior experience integrating complex hardware-software systems in a real deployment context required. To apply, use the form below.

Motion Planning & Controls Engineer

You develop and own the algorithms that determine how the robot moves - from low-level motor control and torque management to high-level trajectory generation and motion planning. You work at the boundary of mathematics and real hardware, ensuring the robot moves precisely, efficiently, and safely in unstructured industrial environments. Experience with control theory, model predictive control, forward and inverse kinematics, and real-time control systems required. Proficiency in Python and C++ required. To apply, use the form below.

Software & AI

Robot Software Engineer

You build the intelligence layer that sits above the hardware - navigation and motion planning, task execution, fleet coordination logic, and the command interface that lets operators give the robot plain-language instructions. You work in ROS 2, Python, and C++, and you care deeply about making robot behavior reliable in real, unpredictable environments. Experience with autonomous navigation, motion planning, or task execution systems required. To apply, use the form below.

Perception & Sensor Systems Engineer

You build the robot's ability to understand its environment. This means sensor fusion across cameras, LiDAR, and IMUs, object detection and tracking, and the localization and mapping stack the robot uses to navigate a warehouse floor. You work at the intersection of hardware and software - selecting and integrating sensors physically, then building the perception pipeline on top of them. Experience with SLAM, OpenCV, and deep learning-based detectors required. To apply, use the form below.

Data

Data Collection Specialist

If you currently perform any repetitive physical work - anywhere from warehouses and factories to repetitive physical tasks at home - we want to talk with you. You wear a mounted camera, perform repetitive workflows across different environments and industries, follow collection protocols, and upload footage to our pipeline. No prior robotics experience required. What matters is reliability and the ability to follow precise instructions consistently. To apply, use the form below.

Robot Operator - Teleoperation

You operate the robot remotely to generate high-quality demonstration data for training our AI systems. You guide the robot through real warehouse and logistics tasks using teleoperation equipment, upload collected sessions to our training pipeline, and provide direct feedback on robot behavior and edge cases encountered during operation. Physical coordination, spatial awareness, and comfort working with hardware required. To apply, use the form below.

ML Data Engineer

You take raw video and sensor data collected from the field and turn it into clean, labeled, model-compatible training datasets. You build and maintain annotation pipelines, define labeling standards, and work directly with the AI team to understand what data the models need and in what format. Experience with video annotation workflows, dataset management, and ML training pipelines required. Familiarity with egocentric video and action labeling is a strong plus. To apply, use the form below.

Operations

Business Development Manager, Industrial Deployments

You identify, engage, and close our first operator partnerships. You understand how decisions get made inside warehouses, logistics operations, and manufacturing facilities, and you know how to navigate them. You source leads, build relationships with operations and procurement stakeholders, structure pilot agreements, and stay involved through deployment to ensure the partnership succeeds. Experience in B2B business development or enterprise sales in an industrial, logistics, or manufacturing context required. An existing network of operator contacts is a strong advantage. To apply, use the form below.

Hardware Procurement & Supply Chain Manager

You source every component that goes into the robot. You identify and qualify suppliers, negotiate pricing, manage lead times, and ensure the engineering team always has what they need to build and iterate without delays. You know the difference between a reliable manufacturer and a cheap one, and you have the relationships that matter. Experience in hardware procurement or supply chain management in an electromechanical or industrial context required. To apply, use the form below.
